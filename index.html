Can Xu

Hello! 👋 I'm Can Xu, a Ph.D. student 🤖 at the TRAIL Lab within the University of Toronto Institute for Aerospace Studies (UTIAS). I am incredibly fortunate to be advised by Prof. Steven Waslander 🌟. and I also work closely with Dr. Mingfeng Yuan.

Before coming to Toronto, I earned my Master’s degree in the ECE at Carnegie Mellon University. There, I served as a research assistant at the AirLab within the Robotics Institute, advised by Prof. Sebastian Scherer, and collaborated closely with Prof. Junyi (Jenny) Geng and and my mentor, Ph.D. candidate Yuheng Qiu. I received my Bachelor’s degree from Beijing Forestry University, where I worked with Prof. Xiangxue Zhang.

My research aims to build robots that can truly understand and interact with the world around them. My current focus centers on building expressive spatial representations, such as dynamic scene graphs. A major goal of my work is advancing human-robot interaction empowering robots to deeply comprehend human instructions and underlying intentions for seamless collaboration. Alongside this, I continue to actively work on robust Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM) systems, visual-inertial odometry, and advanced 3D representations. During my Master’s, my research primarily focused on Learning-Based Inertial Odometry.
